The Australian Healthcare System








I. Global Overview

Average Life expectancy Comparison

Australia's Healthcare system is one of the best in the world. Australians not only avail safe and quality health care but also one of the most affordable health care there is in the world. The Australian Medicare System along with the network of public hospitals provide free or low cost access to healthcare to all Australians. This is one of the main reasons why Australians enjoy one of the longest life expectancies across the globe. Australia ranks 14th amongst 195 countries for the longest life expectancies; This equates to the top 7% of the world which speaks volumes about the robust healthcare system that is established in Australia.

Tip: To see a Country's Life expectancy (in years as of 2019), hover the mouse over the desired country to see a highlighted selection of the country with a tooltip with information.


Healthcare Workers Density Comparison

The density of Healthcare Workers refers to the number of medical healthcare workers there are for every 1,000 people in a country. In 2018, out of 38 member OECD Countries, Australia ranked 9th on the list for the highest Healthcare Workers Density with an average of 68 workers for every 1000 people in Australia.

In Australia, there is a diverse range of Health care providers, such as:

  • Primary care services by General Practitioners
  • Medical Specialists
  • Allied Health Workers
  • Nurses

As of 2018, it's estimated that there were more than 586,000 registered practitioners which includes 98,400 GPs, 330,000 nurses, 20,600 dentists, and 133,000 allied health professionals.

II. Local Affairs

How difficult do Australians find Navigating Healthcare

A survey was conducted by the ABS in 2018 to get an insight into how Australians (18 and over) find navigating the Australian Healthcare system. Survey results show that on average, more than 80% of the Australians find it easy to navigate and get access to healthcare facilities in the Australia. Only a meager average of 14.75% of the people find it hard to avail Healthcare in Australia.


Tip: To observe the individual percentage of each bar, hover the mouse over the bar. Double click outside the chart to revert to normal mode.



Australian Healthcare Expenditures

Since 2001 to 2018, the total expenditure on health care has increased from A$91 billion to $185 which amounts to a growth rate of 4.3% per year. Taking Australia's population into account, the total expenditure on health per person since 2001 has increased by an average of 2.7% per year. Having said this, the expenditure for 2018 seems to be an outlier as it is the only year when the per person spending has dipped by A$27.


Tip: To observe the yearly change in trend for Expenditure based on a minimum Expenditure, use the slider to set the minimum Expenditure.



State and Territories Expenditure Comparison

New South Wales, the most populous state, has always been the highest in expenditure related to Healthcare every year since 2008, closely followed by Victoria, the second most populous state in Australia.

An interesting observation is that the expenditure seems to be increasing on a yearly basis and the ranking order in relation to the amount of statewise expenditure has remained the same since 2008 and it can be noted that this pattern aligns to the population of the state. This can be confirmed by the table accessible on the ABS website.

Tax Revenue & Healthcare Expenditure Comparison

Every year the Australian Government earns revenue from tax. The Government spends a portion of this Tax Revenue to fund the Australian Healthcare system. The general trend that is observed is that the revenue generated from tax has been rising with years except for the years between 2007-11.

However, the Government health expenditure has maintained a constant growth over the years and has demonstrated an overall growth rate of 43% since 2008. This trend is estimated to rise in the present year especially with the uncertainty around COVID-19.


Government and Non-Government Contribution

The 2 charts detail the amount of money spent by the Government and Non-Government entities on different areas of Expenditure in the Healthcare field in the year 2018 - 2019. The Government Funding here represents 2 sources: the Australian Government and the State and local Governments. On the contrary, the Non-Government entities include Health care Insurance companies, Individuals, and other non-government sources.

Out of the A$195 billion spent on Healthcare in Australia, the Government contributed almost 68.2% of the expenditure whereas the non-government entities contributed the remaining 32%.
