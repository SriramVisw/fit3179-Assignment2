The Australian Healthcare System








I. Global Overview

Average Life expectancy Comparison

Australia's Healthcare system is one of the best in the world. Australians not only avail safe and quality health care but also one of the most affordable health care there is in the world. This is one of the main reasons why Australians enjoy one of the longest life expectancies across the globe. Australia ranks 14th amongst 195 countries for the longest life expectancies; This equates to the top 7% of the world which speakes volumes about the robust healthcare system that is established in Australia. The Healthcare system is jointly run by different levels of the Australian Government: Federal, State, Territory and local.

II. Local Affairs

How difficult do Australians find Navigating Healthcare

A survey was conducted by the ABS in 2018 to get an insight into how Australians (18 and over) find navigating the Australian Healthcare system. Survey results show that on average, more than 80% of the Australians find it easy to navigate and get access to healthcare facilties in the Australia. Only a meagre average of 14.75% of the people find it hard to avail Healthcare in Australia.

Australian Healthcare Expenditures

Since 2001, the total expenditure on health care has increased from A$91 billion to $185 which amounts to a growth rate of 4.3% per year. Taking Australia's population into account, the total expenditure on health per person since 2001 has increased by an average of 2.7% per year. Having said this, the expenditure for 2018 seems to be an outlier as it is the only year where per person spending has dipped by A$27 per person.

State and Territories Expenditure Comparison

As can be noted from the previous graph, the expenditure has seen to rise every year. New South Wales, the most populous state, has always been the highest in expenditure related to Healthcare every year since 2008, closely followed by Victoria, the second most populous state in Australia. An interesting observation is that the expenditure seems to be increasing on a yearly basis and the ranking order in relation to the amount of statewise expenditure has remained the same since 2008 and it can be noted that this pattern aligns to the population of the state. This can be confirmed by the table accessible on the ABS website.

Government and Non-Government Contribution

The 2 charts show the amount of money spent by the Government and Non-Government entities on Healthcare expenditures in the year 2018 - 2019. The Government Funding here represents 2 sources: the Australian Government and the State and local Governments. On the contrary, the Non-Government entities include Health care Insurance companies, Individuals, and other non-government sources. Out of the A$195 billion spent on Healthcare in Australia, the Government contributed almost 68.2% of the expenditure whereas the non-government entities contributed the remaining 32%. The 2 charts detail the amount of money spent on different areas of Expenditure in the Healthcare field which is expected to increase in the coming years.
